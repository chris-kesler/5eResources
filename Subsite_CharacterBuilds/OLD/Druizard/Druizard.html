The Meta

The Druizard is an interesting concept I came up with recently that makes good use of the new Druid Circle released in Tasha's Cauldron of Evertyhing: The Circle of Stars. I will go on record and say that I think that build should be called the Druizard. Druid + Wizard = Druizard. It also sounds a bit like Charizard which is fitting since the constellation it relies on is the Dragon.

Rather than wildshaping, you can expend a use to take on a starry form. Three starry forms are available, with one dealing some extra damage and another offering extra healing. However, the one that caught my eye was the Dragon constellation. While this form is active, you can treat any roll on the d20 of 9 or lower as a 10 as long as you are making an Intelligence Check, Wisdom Check, or Concentration Saving Throw.

This is amazing just for Concentration purposes, but the real catch is the bit about Intelligence Checks. I've made Wizards in the past who get Arcana Expertise and use a Mizzium Apparatus to avoid preparing low level spells, but they will dwarf compared to the Druizard. A two level starting dip in Stars Druid followed by full Wizard with Arcana Expertise and a Mizzium Apparatus is the foundational concept for the Druizard.


The Concept

I love the theme behind nature magic. Druids are probably the coolest caster in terms of spell theme. However, I greatly prefer to play Intelligence casters due to personal bias. Druids use Wisdom, making them good at Animal Handling, Insight, Perception, etc. But what about a Druid that uses Intelligence? Where do the book smarts come from if this character is so connected to nature? I've decided to make the Druizard something of a biologist, a researcher making a compendium of all things in the natural world and spending most of his time in the field. The Druid limitations for Armor can be explained as "too cumbersome to move about in" rather than a religious or spiritual reason.
