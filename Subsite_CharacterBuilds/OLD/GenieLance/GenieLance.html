  • The Meta

  • I have had the pleasure of reading an old and highly influential character build known as the Forcelance. The original build went Vuman (Variant Human) with PAM (Polearm Master) and the Hexblade Patron, taking Warcaster at level 4 and CBE (Crossbow Expert) at level 8. The primary tactic used by the Forcelance was holding a Quarterstaff so that enemies would trigger OAs (Opportunity Attacks) upon entering their reach. The neat part is that Warcaster let them cast EB (Eldritch Blast) instead of taking that OA. Couple this with AB (Agonizing Blast) and RB (Repelling Blast) and you have a serious boost to your ability to spam EB. The Forcelance ends up going many levels into Eldritch Knight and other classes as well, but the foundational strategy is PAM and Warcaster for off turn EB.

    I built my own Warlock that takes heavy inspiration from the Forcelance. The primary tactic will involve Warcaster and PAM for off turn EB blasting, but we will be skipping CBE and taking a reach weapon instead. This gives us an extra feat to work with since the reach weapon will trigger OAs at ten feet, meaning EB won't be cast at disadvantage due to close range. We will also be going with the Genie Patron rather than Hexblade, and while this does make Tier 1 play slightly less powerful, it pays off big time in the long run. An interesting thing to note that makes taking something other than Hexblade a bit more bearable is that the Genie Patron does not grant any Armor Proficiencies. There is no decision to be made between Quarterstaff and Shield vs Halberd/Glaive because we can't use Shields. The Hexblade would have to give up Shields to use a reach weapon as a Forcelance, but we don't have that temptation to worry about as a Genielock.
