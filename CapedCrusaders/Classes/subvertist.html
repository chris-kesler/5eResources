Caped Crusaders: Subvertist

Overview

Capable of moving around the battlefield like no other, the Subvertist is a powerful force multiplier and defensive strategist.

Class Features

As a Subvertist, you gain the following Class Features.

Hit Points

Hit Dice: 1d6 per Subvertist level
Hit Points at 1st level: 6 your Constitution modifier
Hit Points at higher levels: 1d6 (or 4) + your Constiution modifier per Subvertist level after 1st

Proficiencies

Armor: None
Weapons: Simple Tech Weapons
Tools: Choose any two
Saving Throws: Intelligence, Wisdom
Skills: Choose any three

Equipment

Armor: Forged Plate, Steel
Weapons: Any Simple Tech Weapon
Tools: One tool with which you are proficient
Packs: One Inventor's Pack

Variant Equipment: Starting Wealth

Rather than acquiring the equipment granted by your class and background, you can elect to purchase your starting gear. If you do so, you receive no equipment from your class and background, and instead roll for your starting wealth using the criteria below:

Techcasting

Subvertist: 1st level
You have gained intimate knowledge of technology far beyond your time, and are capable of apply these technologies in a variety of ways. See the Rules for Techcasting mechanics and Technologies list.

Technologies Known
You learn 4 Technologies of your choice, and you learn an additional 4 at each higher Subvertist level as shown in the Technologies Known column of the Subvertist table. You may not learn a Technology of a level higher than your Max Technology Level, and you may learn a Technology at the same time you learn its prerequisite.

Tech Points You have a number of Tech Points equal to your Subvertist level x 8, as shown in the Tech Points column of the Subvertist table, + your Int modifier. You use these Tech Points to cast Tech Powers. You regain all expended Tech Points when you finish a long rest.

Max Tech Level
Many Technologies can be overpowered, consuming more Tech Points to create a greater effect. You can overpower these abilities to a maximum level, which increases at higher levels, as shown in the Max Tech Level column of the Subvertist table. You may only cast Technologies at 6th, 7th, 8th, and 9th-level once. You regain the ability to do so after a long rest.

Techcasting Ability
Your Techcasting ability is Intelligence. You use your Intelligence modifier whenever a Technology refers to your Techcasting ability. Additionally, you use this ability score modifier when setting the saving throw DC for a Technology you cast and when making an attack roll with one.

Technology save DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Techcasting ability modifier

Technology attack modifier = your proficiency bonus + your Techcasting ability modifier

Augmented Armor

Subvertist: 1st level
Your inventive pursuits have led to you making armor that serves as a conduit for your technology. As part of a Long Rest, you can turn a suit of mundane armor you have access to into Augmented Armor, provided you have Inventor's Tools on hand. You can don or doff the Augmented Armor over the course of 1 minute.
The Augmented Armor has the following traits while worn by you:
  • Your Strength score becomes equal to your Intelligence score as long as it is not already higher. You are also considered proficient with the armor.
  • You can use the Augmented Armor as a Techcasting focus for your Subvertist spells.
  • The Augmented Armor attaches to you and can’t be removed against your will.
  • The Augmented Armor expands to cover your entire body, although you can remove or attach the helmet as an action.
  • The Augmented Armor replaces any missing limbs, functioning identically to a limb it replaces.

The Augmented Armor continues to be Augmented Armor until you create another set of Augmented Armor, or it is destroyed. If you die while wearing the Augmented Armor, it is destroyed.

Subvertist: 2nd level
Choose two of your skill proficiencies and two of your tool proficiencies. Your proficiency bonus is doubled for any ability check you make that uses the chosen proficiencies.

Ability Score Improvement

Subvertist: 4th level
You can increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or you can increase two ability scores of your choice by 1. You can’t increase an ability score above 20 using this feature. You may gain this feature multiple times. Using the optional feats rule, you can forgo taking this feature to take a feat of your choice instead.

Reverse Engineering

Subvertist: 6th level
Choose one Technology, Power, or Spell from any class casting list that you have witnessed being cast at least once. Events witnessed within your backstory do not qualify for this feature. The chosen Technology, Power, or Spell must not be of a higher level than the Maximum Technology Level for your current Subvertist level. Your selected Technology, Power, or Spell counts as a Subvertist Technology for you, and you are able to learn it as normal at the same level you choose it. If you are starting at higher levels or have not witnessed any Technologies, Powers, or Spells being cast upon gaining this feature, your GM may provide a list of Technologies, Powers, or Spells for you to choose from. You may gain this feature multiple times.

Ultimate Armor

Subvertist: 20th level
Your inventive pursuits have led to you making armor that serves as a conduit for your technology. As part of a Long Rest, you can turn a suit of mundane armor you have access to into Ultimate Armor, provided you have Inventor's Tools on hand. You can don or doff the Ultimate Armor as part of any Action, Bonus Action, or Reaction you take. In addition, when the Ultimate Armor is doffed by you, you can choose to have it collapse into a small housing unit. The housing unit takes on a form of your choosing, and can be no smaller a thick wristwatch. You can use the housing unit as a Tech casting focus for your Subvertist spells while you wear it as components of the Ultimate Armor dynamically deploy and retract as needed.
The Ultimate Armor has the following traits while worn by you:
  • Your Strength score becomes equal to your Intelligence score as long as it is not already higher. You are also considered proficient with the armor.
  • You can use the Ultimate Armor as a Techcasting focus for your Subvertist spells, and you need not supply any components that do not have a cost while doing so.
  • The Ultimate Armor attaches to you and can’t be removed against your will.
  • The Ultimate Armor expands to cover your entire body, although you can retract or deploy the helmet once per turn.
  • The Ultimate Armor replaces any missing limbs, functioning identically to a limb it replaces.
  • When you make an Intelligence check, you can treat the d20 result as equal to your Intelligence score if the result before modifiers is lower.
  • Choose two of your Technologies that are of 2nd level or lower. You can cast those Technologies at their lowest level without expending any Tech Points.
  • You can survive in the vacuum of space for a number of days equal to your Intelligence modifier. This ability recharges upon a long rest within a hospitable environment.
The Ultimate Armor continues to be Ultimate Armor until you create another set of Ultimate Armor, or it is destroyed. If you die while wearing the Ultimate Armor, it is destroyed.

Subclass: The Virtuoso

A true paragon of invention and ingenuity, the Virtuoso has created and incorporated a truly sustainable energy source. This energy source grants the Virtuoso several at-will Technologies and capabilities, such as flight. As a Virtuoso, you can also convert any Technological Armor you might find into your Subvertist Armor as normal, retaining the original item's benefits in addition to the Subvertist Armor features. (Subvertist Armor refers to Augmented Armor, Advanced Armor, Superior Armor, and Ultimate Armor simultaneously.)

Subclass: The Magnate

Some superpowers are far more subtle than most, and the Magnate is a prime example. Whether relying on magnetic manipulation, electrokinesis, or cybernetics, you stand out as a Subvertist with precise control over your Technology. Having a direct connection between your nervous system and your devices grants you an innate control over your Subvertist Armor. As a Magnate, you can also convert any Metapower Armor you might find into your Subvertist Armor as normal, retaining the original item's benefits in addition to the Subvertist Armor features. (Subvertist Armor refers to Augmented Armor, Advanced Armor, Superior Armor, and Ultimate Armor simultaneously.)

Subclass: The Glyphologist

A scholar of ancient knowledge and mythology, the Glyphologist combines modern science with ancient magical power sources. Whether it be inscribing runes, compressing motes of power, or integrating powerful artifacts, the Glyphologist binds themselves and their armor together on a supernatural level. While not a spellcaster, the Glyphologist still benefits from magical boons that manifest in a variety of ways. As a Magnate, you can also convert any Eldritch Armor you might find into your Subvertist Armor as normal, retaining the original item's benefits in addition to the Subvertist Armor features. (Subvertist Armor refers to Augmented Armor, Advanced Armor, Superior Armor, and Ultimate Armor simultaneously.)
